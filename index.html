?How we keep your data secure+

Protecting your data is a core part of every pickup. We follow the data sanitization guidelines in NIST Special Publication 800-88, and we match the method to the device so your information is always made unreadable:

Phones, tablets & modern computers: These encrypt your data by default. We perform a factory reset, which destroys the encryption key and makes the stored data unrecoverable.

Hard drives: We connect the drive directly and run a secure overwrite that erases the data so it cannot be recovered.

Storage that can’t be reliably wiped: When a device cannot be securely erased, we physically destroy the storage media instead.

Every device is either securely wiped or physically destroyed before it is recycled, resold, or donated. Nothing leaves our hands with your data still on it.

Want proof? A Certificate of Data Destruction is available on request for any pickup.

About the Founder
Hi, I'm Natalie (Chief Rescue Officer)
20 Years Old · Born in Dallas, TX

I am a rising junior at CU Boulder studying economics and political science. During my first semester of sophomore year, I took an Environmental Sustainability and Features course, where I learned about the serious effects of e-waste on our environment when electronics are disposed of improperly.

After researching what options existed in Boulder, I found a real gap. People are left choosing between hauling all their e-waste to a drop-off facility themselves and paying processing fees, or searching to find the best-priced general junk management service that doesn't even specialize in e-waste recycling to come pick it up. I started Juett Tech Rescue to fill that gap: a simple, transparently priced pickup with certified e-waste recycling, and working devices refurbished, resold, or donated instead of shredded, built for Boulder residents. We are proud to be the only waste management service in Boulder focusing specifically on electronic waste management.

The facts are striking: the world generates over 50 million metric tons of electronic waste every year, making it the fastest-growing solid waste stream on the planet. According to the Global E-Waste Monitor 2024, only 22.3% of e-waste is formally recycled globally. When electronics end up in landfills, toxic materials such as lead, mercury, and cadmium leach into the soil and groundwater, posing serious risks to both human health and local ecosystems. The World Health Organization has identified improper e-waste disposal as a growing public health concern, particularly for communities near informal recycling sites. At the same time, rare and valuable metals like gold, silver, and cobalt are permanently lost rather than being recovered and reused.

The goal of this business is to make electronic waste recycling accessible for Boulder County residents by tackling the issue head-on and closing the gap in convenience and affordability. I am also committed to giving back: a percentage of profits from every pickup is donated to Basel Action Network (BAN), a nonprofit dedicated to fighting illegal e-waste dumping and advocating for responsible recycling worldwide.
